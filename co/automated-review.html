Audit Parameters

Audit Frequency Limit

Automated Audits cannot look at claims more than three (3) years after the timely filing deadline. The timely filing period extends 365 days after the date of service.

A provider will receive no more than four (4) Automated Audits per year.

A single Automated Audit is comprised of the following components:

  • The Audit Concept – a short description of what the audit is reviewing, e.g., Behavioral Health Professional Duplicates, EMT Trips to with No Transport, ABA Therapy Initial Assessments Conducted more than Once Per Year, Home and Community Based Services Delivered While Inpatient, Unbundling Laboratory Tests That Should be Bundled, etc.
  • The Audit Scenario – a summary of the audit concept, the provider type(s) impacted, the general rules, policies, and clinical guidelines comprising the criteria that will be used to conduct the audit reviews, specific codes that will be reviewed
  • The percentage of claims reviews per all locations of a provider, identified by the provider billing ID, based on the statutory limits that apply over the course of a calendar year

Monthly Claim Review Limits Determination

Per statute, each month only a percentage of a provider’s claims can be reviewed through Automated Audits (see table below). The exact percentage is based on the total Medicaid reimbursement a provider received in the previous state fiscal year (July 1 – June 30). These limits are updated every year on September 1.

Mailing Limits

The maximum number of Automated audits a provider can undergo for each year must be clearly communicated to providers and reviewed every year by HCPF.
