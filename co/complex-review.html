Audit Parameters

Lookback period for Audits

Complex Audits cannot look at claims more than three (3) years after the timely filing deadline. The timely filing period extends 365 days after the date of service.

  • Claims are subject to RAC audit if the date of service was within the last four (4) years but not within the last 365 days. This time period takes into account the date of service and the additional 365 day timely filing period that the statute states. Claims with a date of service within the last 365 days are still within the timely filing period, but are considered active and not subject to RAC audit.

Examples

If today is August 24, 2025:

  • Claims from August 24, 2021, to August 23, 2024, fall within the RAC audit look-back period.
  • Claims from August 24, 2024, to August 24, 2025, are still within the timely filing period and would not be included in a RAC audit.

NOTE: HCPF may conduct a RAC audit of older claims if required or requested by a federal agency such as the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) or the Office of the Inspector General (OIG), or if there is a potential impact to the Colorado State general fund.

Audit Frequency Limit

A provider cannot will receive no more than three (3) Complex Audits per year.

Complex Audit Definition

A single Complex Audit is comprised of the following three components:

  • The Audit Concept – a short description of what the audit is reviewing, e.g., Readmission, Inpatient Behavioral Health, Inpatient Rehab, Place of Service/DRG, Outpatient EAPG
  • The Audit Scenario – a summary of the audit concept, the provider type(s) impacted, the general rules, policies, and clinical guidelines comprising the criteria that will be used to conduct the audit reviews, and specific codes that will be reviewed
  • The Medical Records Requests related to that Audit Concept and Scenario

Monthly Medical Records Request Mailing Limit Determination

Medical Record Request statutory limits are based on the total Medicaid reimbursement a provider received in the previous state fiscal year (July 1 – June 30). These limits are updated every year on September 1.

The maximum number of Medical Records a provider may be asked for each month must be clearly communicated to providers and reviewed every year by HCPF.
