Exit Conference

When HMS, CO Recovery Audit Contractor (RAC), conducts a Complex Audit involving a review of Medical Records and issues a Notice of Preliminary Findings, a provider may request an Exit Conference to discuss those preliminary findings. Exit Conferences are voluntary, not mandatory, and give the provider, HMS, and HCPF an opportunity to meet and discuss how the preliminary findings were determined, answer any questions the provider may have about the audit or preliminary findings, and review any additional information submitted by the provider in support of the provider’s position with respect to each preliminary finding. For more information on Exit Conferences, please refer to section 25.5-4-301(3.3)(l)(II) of the Revised Statutes (C.R.S.).

How to Request an Exit Conference

If a provider would like to participate in an Exit Conference, the provider must request one within thirty (30) days of receiving the Notice of Preliminary Findings letter. When requesting an Exit Conference, providers must use an Exit Conference Request Form to identify which preliminary findings they want to review at the Exit Conference. Providers can access the Exit Conference Request Form through the HMS Provider Portal or by contacting Provider Services.

Access Provider Portal

To access the provider portal, visit https://hmsportal.hms.com

Register for the Portal

Use the same link and choose Register. On the next screen, select the appropriate box and complete the registration form.

Contact Provider Services

To contact HMS Provider Services, providers should use the following email inbox: CORAC@gainwelltechnologies.com

Scheduling an Exit Conference

An Exit Conference must be scheduled within sixty (60) days of the provider’s request for one. HMS will contact the provider to schedule the Exit Conference at a mutually agreed time and date and will give the provider confirmation once set.

Before an Exit Conference

Providers may submit any additional documentation in support of the questions or claims they want to review at the Exit Conference. Any such additional supporting documentation must be submitted at least 10 days before the Exit Conference to allow HMS and HCPF time to review it.

During an Exit Conference

Exit Conferences will include representatives from the provider, HCPF's clinical staff, and staff from HMS. Providers can discuss preliminary findings with HCPF and HMS, present additional information, engage in clinical discussions regarding questions about the audit and any disputed claims, and attempt to resolve disputes before proceeding to Informal Reconsideration. If HCPF agrees that a preliminary finding was properly billed and therefore should be overturned, that outcome will be reflected in the Notice of Informal Reconsideration. If HCPF and the provider disagree on whether a preliminary finding was properly billed, that outcome also will be reflected in the Notice of Informal Reconsideration (see below).

Following the Exit Conference

All the preliminary findings are overpayments.

Provider Agrees

HMS will issue a Notice of Adverse Action with repayment instructions.

Provider Disagrees

HMS will issue a Notice of Informal Reconsideration (NOIR) within thirty (30) days of the Exit Conference. The Notice of Informal Reconsideration will identify which preliminary findings remain in dispute and which have been resolved. The Notice of Informal Reconsideration also will provide instructions on how a provider can participate in the Informal Reconsideration process.
